EDSA Revolution Day

The EDSA Revolution, also known as the People Power Revolution, was a peaceful uprising that united Filipinos in a fight for democracy. On February 22-25, 1986, millions of people gathered along Epifanio de los Santos Avenue (EDSA) in Metro Manila to demand the resignation of President Ferdinand Marcos, who had ruled the country for over two decades with an authoritarian regime. EDSA Revolution Day is celebrated to honor the bravery, unity, and nonviolent efforts of the Filipino people in reclaiming their freedom.
